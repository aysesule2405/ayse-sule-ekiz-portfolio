Printmaking — Monotypes

Monotype is a one-of-a-kind printmaking process — a single pull from an inked plate, never exactly repeatable. These prints are open edition but each impression carries its own subtle variations.

Rain: Monotype Print #1

Monotype on dry watercolor paper. Sharp-edged rain-like streaks and cut-out shapes. Open edition.

Printed on dry watercolor paper, the ink doesn't bleed or mix. The rough surface creates sharp edges around each shape, giving a cut-out feeling — more graphic and defined than the wet-paper version.

5×7" 8×10" 11×14"

After Rain: Monotype Print #2

Monotype on wet watercolor paper. Saturated color bleeding into fiber — soft and atmospheric. Open edition.

The wet watercolor paper absorbed nearly all of the ink from the plate, resulting in rich, saturated color. Edges of the forms softened as pigments spread into the fibers — a dreamy, atmospheric effect that contrasts with the sharper dry-paper print.

Freeform Monotype

Expressive monotype exploring color, gesture, and spontaneous marks. Open edition.

An experiment in letting go of detailed planning. Layered colors and loose brushstrokes on a smooth plate, transferred in a single pull. The result captures the energy of the process and the way chance shapes the final image.
